<objective>
Derive the apparent flow vector geometry for differential rotation at speed ratio r, compute foil forces at each r ∈ [1.0, 1.5] using the Phase 5/6 NACA interpolator, and classify the force response as multiplicative, additive, or negative relative to the Phase 6 co-rotation baseline at r=1.0.

Purpose: Phase 6 established the Phase 5/6 kinematic baseline at AoA=2° (r=1.0): COP_max=0.944, F_vert=-251.84 N per vessel. The v1.3 question is: if wave pumps co-rotate the water faster than the arm (r > 1), does the shifted apparent flow vector increase horizontal torque (Γ_h) while decreasing the opposing F_vert? Phase 9 answers this kinematically and kinetically. Phase 10 will extend to a full COP(r) sweep.

The key geometric insight: wave co-rotation drives water in the SAME direction as the arm, reducing the foil's relative tangential flow from lambda*v_loop (r=1) to lambda*v_loop*(2-r). This increases the apparent inflow angle beta_eff, raising AoA_eff from 2° toward stall (expected r_stall ≈ 1.31–1.36).
