    TAIL FOIL FORCE BALANCE AND AoA DECISION:

    The tail foil sits at the bottom of the vessel, exposed to the ejected water flowing downward at v_exit(z). The foil generates:
      - Lift L perpendicular to v_exit (i.e., horizontally, not upward unless the foil is angled)
      - Actually: the foil can be oriented so that lift is upward and drag opposes the ejection flow

    The most favorable configuration is the foil oriented so that its lift vector is directly upward (opposing gravity, adding to buoyancy). For v_exit flowing straight down:
      - Foil oriented horizontally with some AoA relative to downward flow
      - Lift = upward force on vessel (beneficial)
      - Drag = force opposing v_exit (opposing ejection, this is the energy cost — but this energy comes from the expanding gas, not from vessel ascent directly)

    For computing W_tail_foil, the WORK done by the tail foil lift over the vessel's ascent:
      W_tail_foil = integral_0^H L_tail(z) dz
    where L_tail(z) = 0.5 * rho_w * v_exit(z)^2 * C_L_3D * span * chord [N]
    and the integration over z accounts for the lift force acting over the height of ascent.

    Note on drag: D_tail(z) = 0.5 * rho_w * v_exit(z)^2 * C_D_total_3D * span * chord [N]
    The tail foil drag opposes the ejection flow (not the vessel ascent directly). The energy to overcome this drag comes from the expanding air pressure. To avoid double-counting, W_tail_foil should use lift only, not (L-D). Include a comment explaining this choice and flag it as a conservative calculation.

    AoA selection for tail foil:
