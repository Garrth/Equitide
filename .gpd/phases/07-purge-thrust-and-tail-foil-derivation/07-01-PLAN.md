    PROP-01 Analytical Derivation â€” Open-Bottom Force Balance:

    The standard Archimedes result for a CLOSED vessel of volume V gives a net upward pressure force F_b = rho_w * g * V. This follows from integrating the hydrostatic pressure over all surfaces.

    For the OPEN-BOTTOM vessel, the bottom face is open: there is no pressure force on the bottom face from the water. The net upward pressure force on the vessel hull is therefore:

      F_pressure_hull(z) = rho_w * g * V_air(z)

    where V_air(z) = V_surface * P_atm / P(z) is the air volume at height z (Boyle's law). This equals the standard Archimedes force because the bottom face (open to water) contributes zero net force â€” the water freely enters/exits. So:

      F_b(z) = rho_w * g * V_air(z)    [same as standard Archimedes result]

    Now consider the momentum argument. As the vessel ascends at v_vessel, air expands and water is ejected downward through the open bottom. The ejected water carries downward momentum. By Newton's third law, the reaction force on the vessel is upward. However:

    The rate of change of downward momentum of the water BELOW the vessel is:
      d(p_water_below)/dt = F_net_on_water_below = P(z)*A_opening - rho_w*g*(H-z)*A_opening - (weight of water column above)

    This is exactly the hydrostatic pressure difference that drives the standard buoyancy calculation. The momentum carried by the ejected water is already accounted for in the hydrostatic pressure integral on the vessel hull. The pressure force on the vessel's upper faces (which does not cancel because the top is closed) already "sees" the expansion.

    RESOLUTION (PROP-01): The ejected water momentum transfer is NOT a separate force from the pressure-based Archimedes force. Both descriptions â€” the Archimedes pressure integral and the momentum ejection argument â€” describe the SAME physical interaction: the hydrostatic pressure of the water column acting on the enclosed gas volume. F_jet as a SEPARATE additive force would double-count Pitfall C6.

    Therefore: W_jet_thrust = 0 as a separate energy contribution. This is consistent with Phase 1 THRM-03. The first-principles justification is: the open-bottom boundary condition does NOT add a new force term beyond the standard Archimedes result, because the standard Archimedes derivation for a variable-volume body already accounts for the pressure force on all closed faces and zero force on the open face.
