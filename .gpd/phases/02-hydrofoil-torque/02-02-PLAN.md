    Load phase1_summary_table.json for W_pump, W_buoy. Do not re-derive Phase 1 quantities.

    Step 1 — Explicit coordinate geometry for tacking sign (MANDATORY — no assumption of symmetry):

    Define global coordinate system for the Hydrowheel loop:
    - Origin at center of loop, viewed from above
    - +x: points to the right (ascending side vessel direction at theta=90°)
    - +y: points toward ascending vessel from center
    - +z: vertical up
    - Positive shaft rotation: counterclockwise viewed from above (vessel travels +x at ascending side)
    - Loop radius R_tank = 3.66 m

    Position A — Ascending vessel at theta = 90° (right side, ascending):
    - Vessel velocity: v_vessel_A = (v_h, 0, +v_v)  [moving right (+x) and up (+z)]
    - Flow velocity relative to foil: v_flow = -v_vessel_A = (-v_h, 0, -v_v)  [flow arrives from right-ahead and below]
    - v_rel = sqrt(v_h^2 + v_v^2)
    - Flow direction unit vector: n_flow_A = (-v_h, 0, -v_v) / v_rel
    - Foil oriented to produce lift in the +x direction (driving rotation)
    - AoA_A: angle between foil chord and n_flow_A; set to alpha_eff from Plan 01
    - F_L_A = F_L * x_hat  (lift in +x direction)
    - Torque on shaft: tau_A = F_L_A * R_tank > 0  (drives +CCW rotation)

    Position D — Descending vessel at theta = 270° (left side, descending):
    - Vessel velocity: v_vessel_D = (-v_h, 0, -v_v)  [moving left (-x) and down (-z)]
    - Flow velocity relative to foil: v_flow_D = -v_vessel_D = (+v_h, 0, +v_v)  [flow arrives from left and above]
    - Without tacking (foil at same fixed angle as ascending):
        - Flow from left-above arrives at the BACK of the foil (wrong side)
        - Lift produced would be in -x direction → tau = -F_L * R_tank < 0 (opposes rotation)
    - WITH tacking (foil angle flipped by tack mechanism, 180° about the span axis):
        - Foil leading edge now faces the flow from left-above
        - NACA 0012 at AoA alpha_eff (same |alpha| due to symmetric profile):
            C_L_3D(+alpha) at +alpha = C_L_3D(-alpha) at -alpha = same magnitude
        - Flow (v_h, 0, v_v) → flow impinges from left-and-above → lift perpendicular to flow
        - Direction of lift after tack: perpendicular to (v_h, 0, v_v) in the x-z plane
          = perpendicular to (v_h, 0, v_v) rotated 90° toward +x direction
          = (+v_v, 0, -v_h) / v_rel normalized
        - The x-component: F_L_x = F_L * v_v / v_rel > 0  (positive x direction!)
        - Torque: tau_D = F_L_x * R_tank > 0  (drives SAME rotation as ascending — verified!)

    Explicitly compute and report in code:
    - F_L_A_x / F_L = v_v / v_rel  at ascending (should equal sin(alpha_water))
    - F_L_D_x / F_L = v_v / v_rel  at descending after tacking (should be same magnitude)
    - Both are > 0 → tacking CONFIRMED: both contribute positive torque

    Cross-check: This is identical to the Darrieus VAWT result where the tangential force
    coefficient C_T = C_L * sin(phi) - C_D * cos(phi) is positive on both upwind and downwind
    passes when L/D > 1/tan(phi) where phi is the inflow angle. Verify this condition is
    satisfied at the design point.

    Step 2 — NACA 0012 symmetry verification:
    From NACA TR-824: C_L(+alpha) = -C_L(-alpha) for symmetric profiles (odd function).
    C_D(alpha) = C_D(-alpha) (even function).
