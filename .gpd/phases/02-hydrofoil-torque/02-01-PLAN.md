    **CRITICAL: Resolve the chain loop geometry FIRST. If v_h = 0 on the ascending section, output a STOP verdict and halt this plan.**

    Step 1 — Chain loop geometry and v_h derivation:
    The Hydrowheel loop is a conveyor: vessels travel up one side of the tank, over the top sprocket, down the other side, under the bottom sprocket, and repeat. The tank radius R_tank = 3.66 m; H = 18.288 m.

    Analyze two geometry models and compute v_h for each:

    Model A — Curved-arc only (conservative base case): The ascending and descending straight sections are purely vertical (like a bucket elevator); v_h = 0, v_v = v_chain on the straight runs. The foil generates useful shaft work only in the curved sprocket sections. For a sprocket of radius r_sprocket (estimate r_sprocket ≈ 0.5 m as a reasonable default), the curved section at each end subtends a half-circle: arc_length = pi * r_sprocket. As the vessel traverses the arc at angle theta from vertical, v_h(theta) = v_chain * sin(theta) and v_v(theta) = v_chain * cos(theta). The tangential force contribution is F_tang = F_L * sin(theta). Integrate P_net(theta) = F_tang(theta)*v_h(theta) - F_D*v_rel over theta in [0, pi] for both top and bottom sprockets.

    Model B — Angled straight sections: If the ascending run is inclined at angle phi from vertical (phi > 0 means angled outward), then v_h = v_chain * sin(phi) directly on the straight section. This gives v_h > 0 over the entire ascent path. The straight section fraction is f_straight = (H - 2*r_sprocket) / H. Compute W_foil over the full ascent (curved arc contribution from Model A plus straight section contribution with v_h = v_chain * sin(phi)). Run phi as a free parameter to identify the minimum phi (loop inclination angle) required to achieve COP=1.5.

    Recommended approach for Phase 2 feasibility: Adopt Model A as the BASE case (most conservative) and compute W_foil over the curved arc only. Also run Model B with phi as a free parameter. Note: Model B with phi = 0 reduces to the straight sections contributing W_foil = 0, recovering Model A's straight-section result — they are consistent at the phi = 0 limit.

    If ANY model gives v_h > 0 over a meaningful fraction of the path, proceed. If ALL models give v_h = 0 (purely vertical straight chain, no curved arc contribution), output STOP condition.

    Define v_h_model_A for the curved-section-only analysis:
    - On the curved arc (angular range from 0 to pi around sprocket):
      theta = angle from vertical at sprocket; v_h(theta) = v_chain * sin(theta)
    - Integrate P_net(theta) = F_L(theta)*v_h(theta) - F_D*v_rel(theta) over theta in [0, pi]

    Step 2 — Velocity triangle and AoA:
    At each position with v_h computed from Step 1:
    - v_rel = sqrt(v_v^2 + v_h^2)
    - alpha_water = arctan(v_v / v_h)  [angle of incident flow BELOW horizontal]
    - alpha_eff = alpha_mount - (90° - alpha_water)  [angle seen by foil relative to v_rel]
      where alpha_mount is the foil mount angle measured from the vessel long axis (horizontal)
    - For positive lift in the rotation direction, need alpha_eff in [5°, 10°]
    - Solve for alpha_mount = alpha_eff + (90° - alpha_water) for chosen alpha_eff at the design point

    DO NOT set alpha_eff = alpha_mount. Always compute from velocity triangle (Pitfall m2 guard).

    Step 5 — Prandtl LL finite-span correction (MANDATORY):
    e = 0.85 (Oswald efficiency, rectangular foil)
    C_L_3D = C_L_2D / (1 + 2/AR)
    C_D_i = C_L_3D**2 / (pi * e * AR)

    Step 6 — Force and power computation:
